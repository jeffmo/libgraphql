Concept 3: Graph-Edge Polygons

"Graph" is already in "GraphQL." The Graph-Edge Polygons system builds each crate's badge from vertices and straight-line edges -- the fundamental primitives of graph theory. Every glyph is a wireframe polygon with visible vertex dots, evoking engineering diagrams and schematic blueprints.

Complexity scales with scope: the parser gets the simplest shape (triangle, 3 edges), macros get a diamond (4), core gets a pentagon (5), CLI gets a chevron (5, directional), and the apex libgraphql crate gets the most complex heptagon (7 edges) with faint interior cross-edges that hint at the rich interconnections of a full graph engine.

  • Rooted in graph theory -- vertices and edges are the visual language
  • Complexity hierarchy maps naturally to crate scope (3 to 7 edges)
  • Stroke-only wireframe aesthetic reads cleanly at every size, from 16px favicon to banner
  • Each shape has a unique silhouette -- instantly distinguishable even at small sizes
  • Extensible: new crates get a new polygon (hexagon = 6, octagon = 8, etc.)
